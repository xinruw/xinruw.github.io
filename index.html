Curriculum Vitae

Hi. I'm Xinru Wang.

I’m a second year Ph.D student in the Department of Computer Science, Purdue University, fortunate to be advised by Prof. Ming Yin. My research lies in the fields of human-computer interaction, computational social science, and behavioral sciences. I'm especially interested in exploring people's interaction with AI via both experimental and computational approaches.

Prior to Ph.D, I received my bachelor degree from Peking University, China.

Projects

Here are some interesting projects that are ongoing or have been completed.

Human Behavior Models in AI-assisted Decision Making

This project proposes a space of three-component models (i.e. inference + utility + selection) that resemble human behavior in the setting of AI-assisted decision making.

Compatible Update of AI Explanations

We provide a starting point of how the model explanation will change due to a model update, and how this change may influence people’s trust in AI or team performance.

Effects of AI Explanations

We present a comparative study to understand the effectiveness of four types of XAI methods in supporting people to make better decisions. This work has been accepted as a long paper in IUI'2021.

Chinese Ink-Wash Painting Style Rendering

Final project of CS535 on 2019/5. A GPU-based Chinese ink-wash painting style rendering program for any 3D animal models.

Robo Bar

Course project of Introduction to Artificial Inteligence on 2017/12. A speech-controlled restaurant-service robot.

MYOTee

Minesweeper
